Core Capabilities

What the System Offers

Six integrated modules that bring verifiable transparency to the entire Ayurvedic herb supply chain.

01
GPS Geo-Tagging
Precise coordinates are recorded at the point of herb collection — capturing altitude, forest zone, seasonal timestamp, and collector identity on-chain.
02
Immutable Ledger
Every custody transfer, quality test result, and processing event is written to a permissioned blockchain — tamper-proof and auditable by all stakeholders.
03
Authenticity Verification
QR codes on final product labels resolve to a full provenance report — consumers and regulators can verify species, origin, and processing history instantly.
04
Smart Contract Compliance
Automated smart contracts enforce AYUSH quality thresholds, triggering alerts or batch holds whenever lab results fall outside permitted parameters.
05
Biodiversity Mapping
Aggregated collection data generates live density maps of medicinal plant populations, supporting conservation policy and sustainable harvesting quotas.
06
Multi-Stakeholder Access
Role-based access for collectors, processors, manufacturers, certifiers, and consumers — each with a scoped view of the shared ledger appropriate to their function.
Methodology

From Forest to
Formulation

1
Field Collection & Geo-Tag
Collector scans a field app; GPS coordinates, species ID photo, and timestamp are hashed and submitted as a blockchain transaction.
2
Drying & Primary Processing
Processing facility logs drying method, temperature, and batch weight. NFT token minted representing the batch.
3
Lab Quality Testing
Accredited laboratory uploads COA results (heavy metals, microbial, active compounds) directly to the chain via oracle bridge.
4
Storage & Cold Chain
IoT sensors record warehouse temperature/humidity at intervals, with anomalies flagged automatically via smart contract.
5
Formulation & Labelling
Final product label QR links to the aggregated provenance record — traceable to the original geo-tagged collection event.
